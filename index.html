Rules

The aim of the game, is to get as high of a score as possible, by matching your dice with one of the available scores!

Below is an example of each score you can get

Left board

Note: if you get a subtotal of 63 or more for the left board, you recieve 35 points extra

ones: 1

twos: 6

threes: 3

fours: 12

fives: 20

sixes: 12

Right board

Note: any selected right board score will return as 0 if you don't meet the scores specifications

three of a kind: 27

four of a kind: 29

small straight: 30

large straight: 40

full house: 25

YAHTZEE: 50

chance: 18

Above are just score examples. When you play, these scores can be anything! The only exception to this rule, as follows:

Small straight: 30 points
Large straight: 40 points
Full house: 25 points
Yahtzee: 50 points

An extra, slightly more advanced part of this game, is to do with when you get a Yahtzee!

So, as you can see from the examples above, a yahtzee is all dice with the same face showing

Now, if you were to get a yahtzee, you would receive 50 points. However, this also brings an extra rule into play

I like to call it the double whammy rule, but this rule is that you get an extra

50 points for getting multiple yahtzee's in 1 game, which means as long as your yahtzee score box

has got 50 points in it, you can get bonus 50 points for each yahtzee you get,

so the earlier you get a yahtzee, the better

Basic Strategies

These are some very basic strategies to the game

  • One of the most important strategies in Yahtzee!, is using the dice holding feature!
    To use this feature, you just have to click on one of the dice, and a red border will show around the selected dice.
    Here are some examples below:
    • 2 rolls remaining this turn

      1 rolls remaining this turn

      0 rolls remaining this turn

      four-of-a-kind: 29

    Note: when you have no rolls left, holding any dice is useless, as your next turn after selecting a score, will remove the dice hold anyway

Advanced strategies

Here are some slightly more advanced strategies for players to work with!

  • A useful strategy to the game yahtzee, is using the chance score, when you have a low score for a high dice number, like fives or sixes.
    Here's an example of using this strategy below, with both sixes and chance score being shown:
    • 0 rolls remaining this turn

      Sixes: 12

      Chance: 18

    Since you have only 2 sixes, your best chance of getting the bonus score, is by trying to get 3 of a kind with sixes or better.
  • Another good strategy to use, is choosing the ones when you have nothing else to go for, like a straight.
    Here's some examples:
    • 0 rolls remaining this turn

      ones: 1

    In this example, you're trying to get a large straight, and only a 3 would get it for you. In some cases, like this, picking chance when you have ones available isn't a great move.
    This is because chance is very useful for getting you out of situations where you have absolutely no scores to get.
  • The next top strategy you can use, is when you have used all of the scores on your left board, or if the dice available to you doesn't give you a left score.
    This strategy is highly risky, since it gives you a score of 0, and also removes the chance of getting any extra 50 points for multiple yahtzee's in a game.
    If nothing is available to you on the right board as well, you can use the yahtzee score, if it's still available, to be able to get points for the easier scores.
    Here's an example of it in use, with all of the left board, both straights already used and chance already used:
    • 0 rolls remaining this turn

      YAHTZEE! 0

    Since yahtzee is the hardest score to get, it's highly unlikely that you'd get a yahtzee, so to maximize your score in the situation stated above, you should go the yahtzee score.
    You should then go down by score difficulty, which would be large straight, small straight, full house, four of a kind, three of a kind, in order of score difficulty.
    This would depend on what scores you have already selected.
